Morality

Overview

Morality is simply put the measure of a Force characters Light Side and Dark Side Rating, Good vs Evil, Right and Wrong. This value changes reflecting on a character’s choices and actions as an adventure progress.

Process

Players can choose from the table below, while most of these Strengths and Weakness pair well together you can feel free to pair something that fits your character well. Also, if you feel like a strength and weakness that isn’t listed feel free to make your own with GM approval and the idea is fully fleshed out. Your character will start at 50 Morality.

Morality in Play

During gameplay, each player is responsible for tracking his PC’s Morality at the end of each game session. A Pc’s actions during a session may increase the probability that their morality will rise or fall. Whether a PC increases or decreases is determined through conflict. Conflict is a resource that PCs accumulate throughout a game session, the more conflict a PC accumulates, the greater the chance his morality will decrease at the end of the session. Conflict is tracked publicly by each PC player and may be tracked on the sheet or other spots. These are the following ways that generate conflict automatically:

  • Using dark side results to generate force points when activating a force power or force talent.
    • Any time a character uses one or more dark side results to generate force points he gains 1 conflict per dark side result.
  • Performing certain narrative actions
    • You can also gain conflict for performing immoral actions as determined by the GM. Extreme actions, including taking lives can give the PC a significant amount of conflict. A GM should always inform a character that they will receive conflict if they perform this action. A GM does not have to reveal how much but can hint at the severity
  • Generating certain results when failing a fear check.
    • When a force Sensitive player fails a fear check the GM can choose to have the PC suffer as much conflict as the difficulty of the check.

Throughout a session, a PC may gain no conflict, 10 or even more. At the end of a session, each player tallies up the conflict his character earned and then rolls a d10. Then subtract the result from the amount of conflict If the result is more then the Character gains that much morality if it is negative then they lose that much. If it is equal no change happens.

Dark Side

If a character’s morality drops below 30, he has crossed the dark side threshold. Being a dark side force user adds significant narrative effects to a character’s ongoing story. These effects are typically determined by the Gm or the Gm and the player. It also has mechanical effects:

  • You generate Force points using Dark Side Results.
    • Instead of using Light Side points they now must use the dark side results. If you would like to use a light side point on a force check, you must flip a destiny point and suffer strain equal to the number of force points generated.
  • The character's presence in the group alters the starting destiny point pool.
    • When in the party, after everyone has rolled for the destiny pool you must flip one destiny token to the dark side.
  • The characters strain threshold may decrease and the wound threshold increase
    • While a character morality score is below 20, the user strain threshold is decreased by 1 and his wound threshold is increased by 1. While they are below 10, they do this again for a total of 2.

When a user crosses back above 30 they are not considered light until they cross onto the light side once again at 70.

Light Side

When character morality increases above 70, they are a true champion of goodness and paragon of the force. When a PC is above 70, they add 1 light side destiny point before characters start rolling. While their morality is 80 and above their strain threshold is increased by 1, and once again when they go above 90.

Career and Specialization

Overview

Upon choosing a Career mark the appropriate career skills and gain 1 rank in one of those 3 skills. Upon picking specialization gain 1 rank in 2 of those skills. Also, when picking a force career, you gain 1 force rating.

Force Powers

Overview

Any character that starts as a force and destiny character will have a force rating of 1, anyone that doesn’t will have to gain one of the universal specializations to become force sensitive. Using the force happens in one of two ways, A user may make a force power check, they roll the force die and use the force points generated either Light side or Darkside, they then use these to activate or enhance the effect as described in the force powers. The other involves them committing a force die. While their force dice are committed to the power (as described in the power) the PC counts his force rating as one lower for each force dice committed.

Process

When making a force power check, the user will roll as many force die as much as there force rating, They generate white or black results. They can then activate the powers results based on their results if they are white results, they can use those freely to activate the powers and other effects this represents them calling on the light side of the force. If the results are black, they can activate these if they must: flip a destiny point to dark side, then suffers strain equal to the number of points he is using, then suffers conflict equal to the number of dark side points he decided to use.

Force Power Purchases and Upgrades:

  • Each force power requires exp to purchase the base power as well as min requirements for Force Ratings.
  • Strength: Strength amplifies the basic effect of the power.
  • Magnitude: Increases the number of target the force power affects.
  • Duration: Duration effects the length of time the power is active.
  • Range: can increase the distance from which the force power can affect targets
  • Control: Add new effects to force powers or modify existing effects.
  • Mastery: Allows you unlock an extremely potent ability for that power.
