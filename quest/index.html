CHARACTER CREATION

Overview

Creating your character is the first part of your journey and playing Star Wars Roleplaying Game by Fantasy Flight Games. This character will likely stay with you for quite some time and leave you with lasting memories. Make sure to consider the consequences of each decision in the process.

Process

You will need to go through each step, keep track of all of this information as it will be important for gameplay. These steps are suggested by the core rulebook in the left to right order listed below but can be dome in any order the player feels comfortable.

Background

Brief

Before you get started it's important to think about what character you’d like to play in your adventure and what type of background you’d like them to have. You want to answer: Where did they come from and what happened that made them into what they are today. Individuals are influenced by a lifetime of decisions and circumstances from birth.

Consequence

Backstories often times fuel the reason a character plays a certain way. This will affect how you as a player make decisions. For instance, if you decide to help an elderly woman because she reminds you of your mother or if you want to attack a group of thugs that are threatening you or buy them a drink. When you find yourself uncertain of what to do in a situation you should fall back on who your character is.

Obligation

Brief

Obligation represents the debt a PC owes. These debts can be physical(Money owed, Services that must be repaid or a binding contract) or they could be intangible(a feeling of responsibility to a friend’s well-being, the duty he feels to help his family or a favor owed to somebody).

Consequence

Generally speaking, this will affect gameplay as much as the player gets involved and develops the character. A player should make decisions for their character so they can fulfill this obligation. A player can obtain new and pay off various obligation throughout gameplay but if a group of PC’s obtain too much Obligation this will affect gameplay as their obligation can affect current missions.

Species

Brief

Species selection brings in a wide variety of possibilities. You will select a species that can help you play out the characters backstory and obligation during gameplay.

Consequence

There are several different consequences when choosing your species. Consider the traits of the character. These affect all actions your character performs. Also you can consider species history, biology and other traits that can affect gameplay.

Career

Brief

When choosing a career, it should complement the character’s backstory, obligation and species. A career is a broad archetype or template of related skills and abilities. Within these are Specializations that help narrow what down they do in the galaxy and give access to special talents and skills.

Consequence

Early on this will have an effect on early game skillset typically a PC excels at 3-4 skills and should take into consideration what this character should be good at. As throughout the course of the game a PC can acquire new specializations, talents and gain more experience in skills.

XP / Credits

Brief

Spending XP and Credits is where you shape your character further with which gear, talents, skills and weapons they will start with.

Consequence

How a character spends XP will affect the base stats of a species allowing a player to specialize in a certain style or play. Subsequently what weapons and gear a player purchases will affect how well they perform in different tasks like survival and combat.
